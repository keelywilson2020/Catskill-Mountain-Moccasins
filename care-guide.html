Wear & Care

Wear Instructions

Your moccasins are handmade from natural materials and designed to mold to your feet over time. Follow this guide to ensure a proper fit, long-lasting comfort, and years of wear.

Step 1: Unbox & Admire

Take your moccasins out of the box and unlace them if needed. Hold them in your hands and say, "Wow, they're even nicer than I expected!" You're about to begin a beautiful journey.

Step 2: Try Them On

Remove socks or stockings for your first try-on—bare feet make it easier.

The first time may feel snug. That's intentional. The leather will stretch and mold to your feet. Go slow.

Important: Only pull from the front trim and the lower back (see Figure 2). Do not pull from the sides.

If you're stuck, place your foot on the floor, wiggle your toes, and gently inch your foot forward while pulling in the recommended spots. After a few wears, they'll slide on with ease.

Step 3: Lacing Instructions

Once your foot is centered and fully inside:

  1. Start with the bottom button—this one must be laced tightly to prevent your heel from slipping out (see Figure 3).
  2. Pull the lace from the top of each heel tab over the top of each button, working your way up.
  3. When you reach the top, cross the laces as shown (Figure 4), make a loop, tuck it between the lace and button, and pull it snug.
Pro tip: Oil your laces for smoother, easier lacing.

Care Instructions

Leather Conditioning & Cleaning

Treat your moccasins before wearing them outdoors, especially in wet or cold weather. Reapply treatment whenever they feel dry or stiff.

We Recommend:

  • Bick4® – cleans, conditions, and repels water.
  • Neatsfoot Oil® – great for deep conditioning in warm seasons.
  • Mink Oil (no silicone or wax) – waterproofs and insulates for colder months.

Apply by rubbing deeply into the leather.
For suede areas, use Gard-More® spray instead—this protects without darkening.

Never use petroleum, wax, or silicone-based products—they clog leather pores and shorten the life of your moccasins.

Washing Your Moccasins

Your moccasins are machine washable once or twice per year:

  1. Remove laces and wool liners.
  2. Wash in cold water, gentle cycle, with Woolite® or castile soap.
  3. Air dry out of direct sunlight.
  4. Before they're completely dry, apply Neatsfoot Oil® or Bick4®.
⚠️ DO NOT machine wash moccasins with:
  • Light leather and dark trim
  • Beadwork
  • Gemstone buttons
When in doubt, call us: 845-679-7302

Sheepskin Liner Care

When liners get flat or funky (usually every 20–30 wears):

  1. Remove them by lifting the heel and working your fingers to the front.
  2. Hand wash with Woolite® and lay flat to dry.
  3. Stretch them lengthwise before drying.
  4. To reinsert: roll the liner (fluffy side up) from toe to heel, place it inside, and unroll from heel to toe.

Daily Care & Storage

When you take them off, hang them up to dry. Never leave them in a pile—leather needs to breathe to stay healthy.
